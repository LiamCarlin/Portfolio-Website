§ 04 · Approach

Engineering is how
I think.

01Design systems from first principles, not templates.
02Prototype fast. Instrument everything. Iterate with data.
03Sweat the manufacturing. Tolerances, fasteners, fits.
04Write code where the mechanism needs it. Stay hands‑on.

I build because I want to understand how things actually work — not on paper, but in the shop, on the track, in the air. Most of my projects start with a question I can't answer from a textbook, and end with a part I had to make twice.

What I care about: mechanical systems that are honestly designed, instrumentation that tells the truth, and teams that can still move fast when a bracket cracks at 2 AM. I'm fluent across CAD, machining, controls, and firmware, and I use whichever tool the problem is asking for.

My strongest work sits at the seams — between mechanical and electrical, between driver feel and sensor data, between a clean CAD file and a part you can hold. That's where I want to keep working.
